# Riverbend Site Environmental Questionnaire — 2025

**Site:** RBC-01 — Portland Bottling Plant (flagship)
**Completed by:** Denise Kowalczyk, Plant Manager (23 years at Riverbend)
**Date:** January 2026

**1. Describe your site's main energy uses.**
Bottling lines 1-6 (line 6 added 2023), blow molding for PET, CIP/sanitation hot water
(natural gas boilers, two of them, installed 2011), compressed air, chillers for product
cooling, warehouse lighting. We run two shifts, five days, plus weekend sanitation.

**2. How is energy use recorded at your site?**
Utility meters, billed monthly by Portland General Electric and NW Natural. I keep every
invoice going back to at least 2018 in binders in my office — happy to pull whatever you
need. We also have submeters on lines 4-6 from the 2023 expansion but honestly nobody
looks at them.

**3. Any on-site fuel storage or combustion besides natural gas?**
One diesel backup generator (500 kW), tested monthly, maybe 300-400 gallons/year. Two
propane forklifts left in the warehouse, rest of the fleet is electric now.

**4. Refrigerants used on site?**
The chillers use R-134a I believe, maintenance contract is with Cascade Mechanical, they'd
have the service records. We had a recharge done in 2025 after a leak on chiller #2.

**5. Water use?**
Big topic here. Roughly 1.8 gallons of water per gallon of finished product, city water.
Corporate has talked about a water target for years. Nothing formal yet.

**6. Planned changes that would affect energy/emissions?**
There's a capital request in for rooftop solar (submitted 2024, still pending). Boiler
replacement is on the 5-year plan — the 2011 units are getting tired.

**7. Anything else we should know?**
You should talk to whoever handles the Headwater plants separately — their reporting
doesn't come through our systems. Also, our October numbers got submitted twice to
corporate because of the reporting-portal migration; I flagged it to finance but never
heard back whether it was fixed.
