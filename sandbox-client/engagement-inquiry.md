# Engagement Inquiry — Riverbend Beverage Group

**From:** Rosa Vantia, CFO, Riverbend Beverage Group
**To:** EY Climate Change & Sustainability Services
**Date:** [Week 1, Day 1 of the cohort]
**Re:** Request for proposal — climate disclosure readiness

---

Our board's audit committee has asked me to get ahead of a problem, and I'd rather buy a
solution than a lawsuit.

Riverbend is a $1.1B beverage group — bottling, an acquired craft-brands portfolio,
packaging operations, and our own cold-chain distribution. Four things have landed on my
desk in the last two quarters:

1. Counsel advises we are in scope for **California SB 253** and should treat the first
   reporting deadline as fixed.
2. **Summit & Vale Markets** — roughly 30% of our revenue — has added emissions reporting
   to its 2027 vendor requirements, including what their letter calls "value chain
   expectations." I need to understand what that means for us in dollar terms.
3. Our 2025 refinancing included a **sustainability-linked facility**. There is a margin
   step-up if we miss the reporting covenants. My treasury team can quantify the step-up;
   nobody can tell me our likelihood of missing it.
4. In 2024, before Marcus joined us, our marketing team ran a campaign stating Riverbend
   will be **"carbon neutral by 2030."** The audit committee asked me what supports that
   claim. The honest answer today is: nothing I would sign.

What we have: utility invoices scattered across 17 operating sites, a fleet system export
nobody has ever analyzed, site managers who know their buildings well but record little,
and an acquired division (Headwater Brands) whose systems we haven't integrated. Marcus
Oyelaran, our Director of Sustainability — our first — has started collecting files and
will be your day-to-day contact. He is capable and completely under-resourced.

What I want from you, within roughly a month:

- A view of **what climate data we have and whether any of it can be trusted** — and a
  specific list of what to collect that we currently don't.
- A **defensible greenhouse-gas inventory** for what can be calculated now, presented so
  that my board can read it — I care about which sites, which divisions, and what it
  costs us, not the chemistry.
- Your view of **what actually matters financially**: which climate issues move our costs,
  our revenue, and our lender covenants, ranked in dollars, with what you'd do about each.
- A frank assessment of **the 2030 claim** — what would substantiating it require, and
  what is our exposure if we keep saying it.

One warning, because I've bought sustainability work before: if your deliverable reads
like it could have been written for any company, I will notice, and it will be our last
engagement. Riverbend runs on specifics.

Please come back with a short proposal: what you'll deliver, in what order, what you need
from us, and how your team works. Marcus will send the data files to whoever you assign.

Rosa Vantia
Chief Financial Officer, Riverbend Beverage Group
