03
The Contact

Rosa Vantia, CFO

Who she is

Third-generation family. Owns the lender relationship and the audit committee. Famous internally for asking "what does that cost us?" in every meeting.

Your ally

Marcus Oyelaran, Director of Sustainability — hired 8 months ago, the company's first. Capable, under-resourced, and the one sending you the files.

Rosa speaks money, not sustainability. Every deliverable is judged by one test: would Rosa forward it to her board?

04
The Ask

Four things landed on Rosa's desk

  • California SB 253 — in scope; counsel says treat the deadline as fixed.
  • Summit & Vale Markets (30% of revenue) now requires supplier emissions data via CDP — including value-chain expectations.
  • A sustainability-linked loan with a margin step-up if reporting covenants are missed.
  • "Carbon neutral by 2030" — a 2024 marketing claim the audit committee just asked her to substantiate. Her words: "the honest answer today is: nothing I would sign."
05
The Warning
"If your deliverable reads like it could have been written for any company, I will notice, and it will be our last engagement. Riverbend runs on specifics."
— Rosa Vantia, engagement inquiry

She has bought sustainability work before. Generic is the failure mode — for her consultants, and for AI output. Specifics are the whole game.

Your Assignment

Build the proposal

Rosa asked for a short pitch. That's your job this week:

  • Proposed deliverables — what will you produce in four weeks, in what order?
  • Benefits and outcomes — connected to her four problems, in her language: dollars, deadlines, covenants.
  • Data requirements — what do you need from Marcus and the sites? Be specific.
  • How an AI-native team works — speed, method, and why she should trust output built this way.
