Why Disclosure Is Hard
Understanding the problem is what makes you good at solving it
Regulatory complexity
TCFD, CSRD, SEC rules — each framework has different datapoint requirements, different materiality logic, different defensibility standards. Companies often don't know what they need until they're already late.
Data coordination
ESG data lives across portcos, finance teams, and ops. Getting it into one place, at the right quality, on deadline is the actual job — not the writing.
Work that doesn't compound
Without a system, every engagement starts from scratch. The reconciliation is the same. The QA is the same. The framework mapping is the same. Year 2 costs as much as Year 1.
The harness changes the third one. You bring the judgment for the first two.

The relationship itself

The Reference Library
Three tiers. Year 1 builds them. Year 2 reuses them.
Process
The engagement playbook. Lifecycle, inputs expected, drafting conventions, QA protocol, version discipline. The master reference. The moat.
Standards
TCFD and CSRD — structure, required disclosures, datapoint maps, common pitfalls, defensible language patterns. One file per framework.
Methodology
Your house style — materiality scoring, data quality rubric, narrative voice. How the firm decides and writes.
Standards are public. Your playbook isn't. That's why the process reference is the one with teeth.
QA Is the Differentiator
Checklist-driven, not memory-driven. The harness runs it every time.
# QA Checklist — TCFD LP Report v1 [ ] All datapoints trace to source (submission or public filing) [ ] Emission factors match methodology ref version [ ] Material topics present in both matrix and narrative [ ] YoY changes > 10% have documented explanations [ ] No numbers in narrative without source in data table [ ] Forward-looking language hedged per SEC guidance [ ] Scope boundaries explicit in every section [ ] Targets stated with baseline year and methodology [ ] Governance section names current board committee [ ] Prior-year figures reconcile to last published report [ ] Sign-off list includes all portco ESG contacts
A LP asks “how do you know this is right?” Your answer: eleven checks, every draft, logged.
Time Compounds With the System
The leverage is in not starting from scratch
Year 1
Build the references while you run the engagement. The upfront investment is in the system, not just the deliverable. Data coordination and interviews take the most time — that stays human.
Year 2+
References reuse. Data collection is incremental. Drafting compresses significantly. The human stages — interviews, sign-off, judgment calls — stay the same.
The references are what make the difference.
A disciplined system built in Year 1 is the asset that carries forward. That's what separates engagements that get easier from ones that stay hard.
