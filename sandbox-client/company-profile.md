# Riverbend Beverage Group — Company Profile

> **Note:** Riverbend Beverage Group is a fictional company created for training purposes.
> All data is simulated. Any resemblance to real companies or persons is coincidental.

## Overview

| | |
|---|---|
| Founded | 1958, Portland, Oregon |
| Ownership | Family-held (Vantia family, 74%); minority stake held by Larch Point Partners (PE, 26%, since 2022) |
| Revenue (FY2025) | ~$1.1B |
| Employees | ~4,800 |
| Headquarters | Portland, OR |
| Sites | 22 (17 owned/operated, 3 leased offices, HQ, R&D lab) |

Riverbend is a regional beverage powerhouse of the American West: bottler and brand owner
of carbonated soft drinks, sparkling waters, craft sodas, and functional beverages, with
vertically integrated packaging and its own cold-chain distribution network.

## Divisions

### Riverbend Bottling Co. (RBC) — ~52% of revenue
The legacy core. Five bottling and canning plants producing Riverbend's own brands and
co-pack volume for national labels. Portland (flagship), Sacramento, Boise, Spokane, Reno.

### Headwater Brands (HWB) — ~18% of revenue
Craft and functional beverage portfolio **acquired in 2024** from a founder-led group.
Three plants: Bend OR, Fort Collins CO, Salt Lake City UT. Systems integration is
incomplete — Headwater still runs its own ERP and reports through its own finance team.

### Riverbend Packaging Solutions (RPS) — ~19% of revenue
Packaging division serving both internal demand and external customers. Glass plant
(Tacoma), can sheet (Fresno), corrugated (Salem), PET preforms (Vancouver WA). The Tacoma
glass furnace is the group's single largest energy consumer.

### Coldstream Distribution & Logistics (CDL) — ~11% of revenue
Five refrigerated distribution centers (Portland, Sacramento, Seattle, Denver, Phoenix)
and a fleet of ~240 vehicles, roughly half refrigerated. Serves Riverbend brands and
third-party cold-chain customers.

### Corporate
Portland HQ, Hillsboro R&D lab, and leased sales offices in Chicago, Dallas, and Atlanta.

## Leadership (engagement-relevant)

- **Rosa Vantia** — Chief Financial Officer. Third-generation family. Owns the lender
  relationship and audit committee. Known for asking "what does that cost us?" in every
  meeting.
- **Marcus Oyelaran** — Director of Sustainability. Hired November 2025; Riverbend's
  first-ever sustainability role. Reports to Rosa.
- **Gene Vantia** — CEO. Signed off on the 2024 "carbon neutral by 2030" brand campaign.

## Current situation (why they're talking to us)

- In scope for **California SB 253** disclosure requirements.
- **Summit & Vale Markets** (national grocery chain, ~30% of revenue) has added supplier
  emissions reporting via CDP to its 2027 vendor requirements, including Scope 3
  expectations.
- The 2025 refinancing included a **sustainability-linked loan** with margin step-ups tied
  to emissions reporting covenants.
- Marketing's 2024 **"carbon neutral by 2030"** claim has drawn audit-committee scrutiny —
  no inventory exists to substantiate it.
- No climate data infrastructure: what exists lives in utility invoices, a fleet system,
  site managers' heads, and Headwater's un-integrated ERP.
