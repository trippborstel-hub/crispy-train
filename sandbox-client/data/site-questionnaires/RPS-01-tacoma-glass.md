# Riverbend Site Environmental Questionnaire — 2025

**Site:** RPS-01 — Tacoma Glass Plant (Riverbend Packaging Solutions)
**Completed by:** Sal Iverson, Plant Engineer
**Date:** January 2026

**1. Describe your site's main energy uses.**
The furnace. Everything else is a rounding error. We run a continuous regenerative
gas-fired furnace (rebuilt 2017, next rebuild due ~2029), forming machines, annealing
lehrs, compressors. Furnace runs 24/7/365 — you don't turn a glass furnace off.

**2. How is energy use recorded at your site?**
Gas is metered by Puget Sound Energy, electric by Tacoma Power. Fair warning: our
electrical submetering is from the 1990s and the main meter was replaced mid-2025 after
a dispute with the utility about readings. There was a stretch where we were being
billed on estimated reads.

**3. Any on-site fuel storage or combustion besides natural gas?**
Diesel for the yard switcher and backup pumps, roughly 2,000 gal/year. We also use
oxygen enrichment on the furnace (delivered LOX, not combustion per se but you asked).

**4. Refrigerants used on site?**
Minimal — comfort cooling in the offices only.

**5. Water use?**
Cullet washing and compressor cooling, closed-loop mostly.

**6. Planned changes that would affect energy/emissions?**
The big one: corporate is evaluating an electric or hybrid furnace for the 2029 rebuild.
That decision is worth more than every other efficiency project in this company combined,
and I've been saying so for two years. Also increasing cullet (recycled glass) ratio —
every 10% more cullet cuts furnace energy roughly 2-3%.

**7. Anything else we should know?**
If any number from this site looks insane for a single month, call me before you put it
in a report. Between the meter replacement and the billing dispute, 2025 data has at
least one reading in there I don't believe myself.
