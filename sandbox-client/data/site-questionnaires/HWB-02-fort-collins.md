# Riverbend Site Environmental Questionnaire — 2025

**Site:** HWB-02 — Fort Collins Plant (Headwater Brands)
**Completed by:** Tyler Brandt, Operations Lead
**Date:** February 2026 (submitted late — apologies, we are mid-ERP migration)

**1. Describe your site's main energy uses.**
Brewing and canning for the functional beverage lines. Brew kettles, canning line,
cold storage, packaging hall.

**2. How is energy use recorded at your site?**
Everything goes through the old Headwater finance system still. Rachel in our Denver
office pulls the utility spend monthly. I don't personally see meter data, just the cost
reports. Whatever corporate got, it came from Rachel's export.

**3. Any on-site fuel storage or combustion besides natural gas?**
None — this is a fully electric facility, no gas service on site. The kettles are
electric, which was a big deal when the site was built (2019). It's something the
Headwater brand marketing has always featured.

**4. Refrigerants used on site?**
Cold storage runs on... I want to say R-448A after the 2022 retrofit? The old system was
R-22. Our maintenance is done by a local contractor, I can dig up the service invoices if
it matters.

**5. Water use?**
Metered by the city. Brewing is water-intensive; we track a liters-per-liter ratio
internally for the brand story (currently around 3.1:1).

**6. Planned changes that would affect energy/emissions?**
The ERP migration onto Riverbend's systems is supposed to complete this year, which should
fix the reporting weirdness. There's also talk of consolidating our SLC volumes into Fort
Collins.

**7. Anything else we should know?**
Honest answer: I'm not confident in what got reported for us historically. The unit
conventions in Rachel's exports changed at least once when Headwater switched billing
providers in 2023, and I don't know if anyone downstream ever adjusted for it.
