## What We've Done So Far

We've historically relied on our management consulting relationships — primarily McKinsey — for ESG-related work as part of broader portco operational improvement engagements. The output has been fine but has two recurring problems:

1. **Generic.** The work reads like climate consulting adapted for PE, not PE consulting that incorporates climate. The deliverables don't connect to our value creation plans, our operating review cadence, or the metrics our IC and LPs actually track.

2. **No residual value.** We get a deliverable, it goes in a folder, and when we need an update six months later we're starting from scratch. There's no compounding. No system. Just one-off reports.

We want something different this time.

## What We're Looking For

We have three objectives for this engagement:

### 1. Deliverables with Immediate Operational Impact

We need work product our teams can use — not strategy documents that require a second phase of implementation. Specifically:

- **Fund-level:** A clear picture of where we stand on ESG across the portfolio. What's measured, what isn't, where the gaps are, and what it means for LP reporting and exit positioning.
- **Portfolio company-level:** Company-specific deliverables that connect ESG/climate work to financial outcomes. Emissions baselines, reduction initiatives with cost-benefit analysis, and reporting frameworks the portco teams can maintain.

We care about ESG insofar as it impacts value creation, risk, and exit readiness. We are not looking for a sustainability mission statement.

### 2. A System That Compounds

We've seen your Claude Code-native model. This is the part that interests us most. We want to come out of this engagement with:

- Documented methodologies and reference data our team can reuse
- Templates and playbooks our portco ESG contacts can execute without calling a consultant
- A knowledge architecture that gets smarter with each quarterly update, not one that decays the moment the engagement ends

### 3. A Test Case for AI-Native Consulting

We are an AI-forward firm. We've been evaluating how AI — and Claude Code specifically — can be integrated into our operations. This engagement is partly a test: can a CC-native consulting firm deliver the same (or better) quality as a traditional firm, faster, with better knowledge retention? We want to see it in action.
