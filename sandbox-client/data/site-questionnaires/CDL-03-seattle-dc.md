# Riverbend Site Environmental Questionnaire — 2025

**Site:** CDL-03 — Seattle Distribution Center (Coldstream Distribution & Logistics)
**Completed by:** Priya Raghunathan, DC Manager
**Date:** January 2026

**1. Describe your site's main energy uses.**
Refrigerated warehouse (about 60% of the footprint is cold storage, 34-38°F, plus a
frozen room), dock operations, battery charging for the electric pallet jacks and
forklifts, office space.

**2. How is energy use recorded at your site?**
Seattle City Light monthly billing. Corporate gets the invoices through AP. I see a
monthly cost report, not consumption.

**3. Any on-site fuel storage or combustion besides natural gas?**
Diesel tank (1,000 gal) for the yard truck and the backup generator. The route trucks
fuel at commercial cardlock stations — that all lives in the fleet system, not with me.
Reefer trailers burn diesel too when they're off-grid; I don't think anyone tracks that
separately from tractor fuel.

**4. Refrigerants used on site?**
The main cold storage runs on an ammonia system (installed 2015). We topped off the
ammonia twice this year after minor leaks — service records are with Rainier Refrigeration.
The frozen room addition (2021) runs on R-507A I believe. Truck reefer units are R-404A,
but again, fleet's problem, not mine.

**5. Water use?**
Not significant — sanitation and domestic only.

**6. Planned changes that would affect energy/emissions?**
LED retrofit finished in 2024. There's a proposal for trailer electrification at the dock
(shore power for reefers) that died in budgeting two years running. Denver and Phoenix
DCs are older and worse than us on everything, if you're prioritizing.

**7. Anything else we should know?**
Nobody has ever asked us about refrigerants before this questionnaire, which surprised me
given we're a cold-chain business. If you want leak rates you'll need the service
contractors' records for all five DCs — I doubt corporate has ever seen them.
