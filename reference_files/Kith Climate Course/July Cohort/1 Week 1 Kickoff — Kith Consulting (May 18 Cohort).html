03
The Ask

Sarah has three objectives.

  1. Deliverables with immediate impact. Work product the fund and portcos can use now. Not strategy decks. Not roadmaps. Operational output that connects ESG to value creation.
  2. A system that compounds. Methodologies, playbooks, and templates that persist after the engagement. Knowledge that builds, not decays.
  3. A test case for AI-native consulting. Northwood is AI-forward. They want to see what a CC-native firm can do. Speed, quality, and knowledge retention — compared to MBB.
04
Context

Why Northwood is talking to us.

"The work reads like climate consulting adapted for PE, not PE consulting that incorporates climate."

— Sarah Mitchell on prior MBB engagements
  • Problem 1: Generic. Deliverables don't connect to value creation plans, operating reviews, or the metrics IC and LPs track.
  • Problem 2: No residual value. Every update starts from scratch. No compounding. No system. Just one-off reports.

She's not looking for a sustainability mission statement. She wants ESG tied to EBITDA, risk, and exit readiness.

Your Assignment

Build the proposal.

Sarah asked Kith Consulting to come back with a presentation. That's your job this week.

  1. Proposed deliverables — What will you produce in 6 weeks? Fund-level and portco-level.
  2. Benefits and outcomes — What will Northwood have at the end? How does it connect to value creation and LP reporting?
  3. Data requirements — What do you need from Northwood and the portco teams? Be specific.
  4. Team and approach — How does our CC-native model work in practice? How will we interact with their teams?
  5. Anything else — What should they be thinking about that they haven't raised?
