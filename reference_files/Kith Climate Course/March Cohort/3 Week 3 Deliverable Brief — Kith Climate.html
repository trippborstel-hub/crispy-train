Week 3 — Deliverable Brief

Carbon Inventory &
Emissions Dashboard

Your assignment this week: calculate the Northwood portfolio's carbon footprint and build an interactive emissions dashboard.

Scenario: Northwood's ten portfolio companies have submitted their annual carbon data. This is the first time the firm has had facility-level energy consumption for the full portfolio. The data is in. The methodology is defined. Nobody has run the numbers yet. That's your job this week.

There's also something else in the environment — a McKinsey-designed ESG risk framework commissioned in 2023 and never used. The carbon data you calculate this week feeds directly into it. More on that next week.

Your Five Deliverables

1
Calculate the emissions
Apply EPA Emission Factors (2023) to Scope 1 consumption data (natural gas, diesel, propane, refrigerants) and EPA eGRID (2022) factors to Scope 2 electricity data. Produce mt CO2e for every facility, every portco, and the portfolio total. The methodology is in climate-data-collection-protocol.md. Use it exactly.
2
Build the dashboard
An interactive HTML emissions dashboard. Minimum: portfolio overview (total Scope 1 + 2, portco ranking), portco-level breakdown (Scope 1 vs. Scope 2 split), and a data quality indicator showing Grade A/B/C distribution. Optional: facility-level drilldown, emissions by facility type, geographic view.
3
Assess the data quality
Grade each portco's data A, B, or C per the protocol's quality hierarchy. What percentage of portfolio emissions are calculated from utility bills vs. landlord estimates? Flag specific rows in the CSV that are unreliable — zeros, formula-based estimates, acquisition data in the wrong format. A number you can't trust is not a number.
4
Intensity metrics
Calculate mt CO2e per $M revenue and mt CO2e per employee for each portco. Revenue and headcount are in Supabase — connect to it. Intensity metrics are how Sarah compares Sterling's manufacturing plants to NovaCare's clinics. Absolute emissions can't do that.
5
Scope 3 material flags
You don't have Scope 3 data. That doesn't mean Scope 3 is zero — it means it's unquantified. For each portco, identify the top 2–3 GHG Protocol categories most likely to be material based on their sector and business model. Use the climate questionnaires for context. This is analysis, not calculation.

How to approach this

  • Start with the submitted CSV — it has all 10 portcos, 126 facility rows, Scope 1 and 2 consumption data
  • The emission factors are in the protocol — don't invent your own. Direct Claude Code to read climate-data-collection-protocol.md and apply those exact factors
  • Connect to Supabase for revenue and headcount. The schema is northwood_capital. The portco financial tables have what you need.
  • Cross-reference the CSV with the questionnaires where you find anomalies — zeros, outliers, or values that don't match the described facility
  • The Scope 3 flag is judgment, not math — think about what each business buys, who commutes, and what supply chain exposure looks like for their sector
  • The dashboard should be something you'd show to Sarah — not a data dump

Suggested Output Formats

Emissions Dashboard
HTML with visualizations — portfolio overview, portco breakdown, Scope 1 vs. 2
Intensity Table
Structured output — portco, revenue, headcount, mt CO2e, both intensity metrics
Data Quality Scorecard
Grade per portco — % Grade A/B/C, specific flagged rows
Scope 3 Flags
Table per portco — category, rationale, estimated materiality (high/medium/low)
